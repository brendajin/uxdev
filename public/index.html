Tool #3

Prototyping

Prototype: A first or preliminary model of something

especially useful early on in design sprints - Google uses this as part of their design thinking workshops

“The best way to have a good idea is to have lots of ideas”

- Linus Pauling

founders of quantum chemistry molecular biology
  • The first solution is not necessarily the best
  • debug early, when it matters a lot
  • communicate using data
As you narrow down your product, major changes become more and more expensive - High ROI

Myth

Truth

Prototypes don't have to be

test specific aspects of your design, like look and feel, flow, or practicality. sometimes advantage to test using rougher sketches

Myth

Truth

Prototyping

Make sure you have your pen and paper

Your turn!

You're building a email client for a smart watch with a touch screen. What does the composition window look like?

Spend 1 minute using the sticky to sketch a prototype.

Your turn again!

Sketch a second prototype that is different from the first.

Prototypes are questions, and questions are the currency of collaboration.

We want to test our known unknowns, and discover our unknown unknowns

The goal of rapid prototyping is rapid feedback

test your hypothesis. see how your prototype measures against your goal

To get feedback, you need to test

Work out bugs before you start building - A handful of people will get you the majority of bugs

Direct Observation

  • Ask a tester to talk through thoughts
  • Focus on the goal of problem-solving rather than on your method or interface
  • Use data to evolve your product and your story
Feedback is hard when it comes to your inventions. Don't overreact, pay attention to pain points, don't be defensive, avoid questions like "did you like it?" Ask questions like "how challenging was it to complete the task X?"

“When you prototype something and simulate, you get much better decisions for the next step.”

-Hartmut Esslinger

Founder Frogdesign, created Apple computer brand in the 1980s
